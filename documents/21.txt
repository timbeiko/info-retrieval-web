Definitions
\n
\u201c
CSU
\u201d means Concordia Student Union.
\n
\u201c
DSA
\u201d means Designated Space Administrator.
\n
\u201c
Internal User
\u201d means students and employees of the University with a valid University ID card, all academic and administrative units and all recognized student groups, who require access to and use of University space to conduct their University-related activities.
\n
\u201c
Requestor
\u201d means an Internal User submitting a booking request for temporary use of space on Concordia University property.
\n
A booking request on behalf of a recognized student group must be submitted by a Student Organization Booking Officer recognized by the University.
\n
Reservations by a recognized student group will only be considered if the event date falls within the then-current academic year.
\n
Reserved space may only be used by the individual or group which reserved it. It is prohibited to transfer a reservation to another individual or group without prior notice to, and written authorization
\n
The Requestor understands and agrees that a booking request is subject to the CSU\u2019s prior review and approval and that during this review and approval process:
\n
\u2022 The CSU may, at its sole and absolute discretion, decline an Event or conditionally approve an Event based on the Requestor\u2019s respect of certain conditions.
\n
\u2022 Further information regarding the Event may be required. It is the responsibility of the Requestor to provide the CSU with accurate, complete and up-to-date information within the delay set forth by the DSA, and to inform the CSU of any new or relevant information regarding the Event that may occur after receiving approval from the CSU to hold the Event, failing which the request may be declined or the approval of the Event rescinded, as applicable;
\n
\u2022 Note that any new or relevant information received after approval will entail a revision of the Event and, as such, the Event remains subject to an additional approval;
\n
\u2022 The University may determine that Security is required during the Event, at the Requestor\u2019s cost;
\n
The Requestor recognizes and agrees that:
\n
\u2022 Until the Requestor receives the CSU\u2019s written confirmation that its booking request is approved, the Event will not take place and the space is not booked;
\n
\u2022 The Event location may not be advertised until receiving written confirmation of the booking of the space;
\n
\u2022 The CSU reserves the right to suspend future booking privileges for any Requestor who fails to provide complete and accurate information when submitting the booking request or who fails to update such information as provided in this booking request;
\n
\u2022 It will pay all Event-related costs and fees invoiced to it by the CSU and University;
\n
\u2022 It must and will advise the CSU if registration fees or donation fees are charged to Event attendees, in which case the CSU may, at its sole discretion, charge the Requestor fees, in addition to any other fee payable hereunder, for the use of the Space;
\n
\u2022 It will obtain the University\u2019s Media Relations Department\u2019s written approval prior to inviting media to the Event.
\n
\u2022 The Requestor agrees and undertakes to be responsible for ensuring that all University policies (available at http://www.concordia.ca/vpirsg/policies/), regulations, rules, procedures and terms are followed by the Requestor and his/her/its agents, representatives, employees, officers, directors, governors and invitees, without limiting any Event-related policies, including, but not limited to:
\n
- Policy on the Temporary Use of University Space (VPS-24)
\n
- Policy on the Recognition of Student Organizations and the Use of University Space (VPS-10)
\n
- Security Policy (VPS-20)
\n
- Policy on the Display of Posters (VPIRSG-1)
\n
- Policy concerning Minors on Campus (VPS-8)
\n
Alcohol-related events
\n
If the Requestor wants to serve/sell alcohol at the Event, the Requestor must:
\n
\u2022 Ensure that the Event complies with the Policy on the Sale and Service of Alcohol on University\xa0Premises (VPS-3)
\n
\u2022 Complete and return to the DSA a \u201cWaiver Form for Alcohol Related Events\u201d at least twenty (20) business days prior to the Event. All requests to serve/sell alcohol at the Event may be refused at the sole and absolute discretion of the CSU and shall be subject to such conditions as the\xa0CSU shall communicate to the Requestor as well as to all applicable laws and regulations.
\n
\u2022 Apply to the R\xe9gie des Alcools, des Courses et des Jeux du Qu\xe9bec (\u201c
RACJ
\u201d) for an alcohol\xa0permit at least fifteen (15) days in advance of the Event.\xa0 It is the Requestor\u2019s responsibility to obtain any and all applicable permit(s).
\n
\u2022 It is the Requestor\u2019s responsibility to comply with the Procedures for the sale and service of\xa0alcohol set out in the Waiver, as well as the regulations per the Alcohol Reunion permit.
\n
\u2022 If the Requestor fails to secure the permit(s) from the RACJ, the sale or service of alcohol at the\xa0Event is not authorized by law and by the University.
\n
\u2022 At least two (2) business days prior to the Event, submit to the DSA a copy of all alcohol permit(s)\xa0that are required.
\n
All applicable waiver(s) and permit(s) must be posted and visible onsite at all times during the Event.
\n
Food-related events
\n
If the Requestor wants to serve/sell food at the Event, the Requestor must:
\n
\u2022 Ensure that the Event complies with the Policy on Food or Beverage Service on University Space\xa0(VPS-7)
\n
\u2022 If the Event is catered, include the name of the caterer in the reservation form. Any costs incurred for catering will be paid by the Requestor.
\n
\u2022 If the Event is self-catered, comply with Procedures for Sale or Service of Food on Campus and, if applicable, complete and return to the DSA a \u201cWaiver Form for Self-Catered Events\u201d and comply with the requirements described therein, including requesting and receiving all applicable permits, such as the Special Event permit from the Minist\xe8re de l\u2019agriculture, P\xeacheries et aliminentation de Qu\xe9bec (\u201c
MAPAQ
\u201d). It is the responsibility of the Requestor to obtain all applicable permits.
\n
\u2022 If the Requestor fails to secure any required permits, the Requestor will not be allowed to self-cater the Event.
\n
\u2022 At least two (2) business days prior to the Event the Requestor must submit to the DSA a copy of all food permit(s) that are required.
\n
All applicable waivers and permits must be posted and visible onsite during the Event.
\n
Minors on CSU Property
\n
For the purpose of the present section, \u201c
Minor(s)
\u201d means any person or people under the age of eighteen (18) who is/are not a student(s) of the University. The Requestor must for any Event involving Minor(s) on University space:
\n
\u2022 At least thirty (30) days prior to the Event, the Requestor informs the University\u2019s Insurance Specialist of the Details of the Event by filling out a Declaration of an Activity Involving Minor Children and remitting such completed declaration to the Insurance Specialist;
\n
\u2022 All parents of Minor(s) registering and/or attending the Event must, at least one (1) business day prior to the beginning of the Event, sign and submit to the Requestor, a waiver in favour of the University using the Parental Waiver form.
