Comm, MBA, Juris Doctor<br/>\n<em>President</em></p><p>Soroka has a varied background as a criminal lawyer, professor, union executive, marketing analyst, TV and radio personality, conference speaker, consultant, and, as a pastime, actor and playwright. \xa0He began his career as a marketing and financial analyst for large organizations.\xa0 After earning his Juris Doctor degree (State University of New York) he practiced criminal law. Upon returning to Canada, he served as a\xa0consultant for public and private organizations.</p><p>Soroka is presently a faculty member at JMSB, and was a faculty member at several other universities and CEGEPs. At Dawson College, he served as Department Chair and Program Coordinator for 10 years and founded the annual Dawson Quebec Case Competition. At McGill, Soroka served as the Grievance Officer for the Course Lecturers\u2019 Union.</p><p>He was the recipient of Concordia University JMSB\u2019s Distinguished Teaching Award and the McGill University Management Undergraduate Students\u2019 Teaching Award.</p><p>Soroka has made more than 1500 television and radio appearances (national and local), and was a regular commentator on CTV, Global TV, and CBC. He hosted a local week-in-review radio show for several years.\xa0 One of Soroka\u2019s passions is theatre; he has authored several produced plays.</p><p>As CUPFA president, Soroka provides leadership in all labour-related areas. Among other things, he contributes meaningfully to various committees,\xa0provides strategic direction to the Executive, and manages several portfolios. He contributes his legal and business expertise while serving on the Labour Relations Committee and the Negotiating Team. He is the primary liaison with the University\u2019s Senior Executive, and currently sits on Senate.</p><p>E:\xa0<a href="mailto:robert.soroka@cupfa.org">robert.soroka@cupfa.org</a><br/>\nT: 514-848-2424 ext. 3691</p><hr/><p><strong><img alt="" class="alignleft wp-image-3367" height="236" sizes="(max-width: 154px) 100vw, 154px" src="http://www.cupfa.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/patrice-small-196x300.jpg" srcset="http://www.cupfa.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/patrice-small-196x300.jpg 196w, http://www.cupfa.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/patrice-small.jpg 320w" width="154"/>Me Patrice Blais</strong><br/>\n<em>Vice President, Collective Agreement and Grievance</em></p><p>Patrice Blais has been practicing law since 2006 after obtaining degrees in Law (Universit\xe9 de Montr\xe9al) and Political Science (Concordia University). In 2009, he began teaching at Concordia. He teaches in the fields of business law and ethics, not-for-profit organizations law, labour law, and public security and terrorism in the Departments of Political Science and Management at Concordia; he also teaches business law at Dawson College.</p><p>Me Blais joined the CUPFA Executive in 2010 as VP Grievance and Collective Agreement and was re-elected to that position in 2011, 2014 and 2017.\xa0 As Chief Negotiator for CUPFA, Me Blais negotiated the 4<sup>th</sup>\xa0and 5<sup>th</sup>\xa0Collective Agreements.\xa0 He sits on many University committees, most notably University Senate.\xa0 Me Blais is committed to ensuring that the contributions of part time faculty are recognized and their working conditions bettered, and that the governance of the university is improved.<br/>\nE:\xa0<a href="patrice.blais@cupfa.org">patrice.blais@cupfa.org</a><br/>\nT: 514-848-2424 ext. 3691</p><hr/><p><strong><img alt="" class="wp-image-1187 alignleft" height="119" src="http://www.cupfa.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/loraine_bio1.jpg" title="loraine_bio" width="119"/>Lorraine Oades</strong><br/>\n<em>Vice President, Professional Development</em></p><p>Lorraine Oades joined the CUPFA executive as Vice President of Professional Development in 2012. A practicing artist with over 22 years of teaching, she has acquired a broad range of experience from within the university and the Montreal art community.</p><p>Oades has been actively involved on many committees at Concordia, including Senate, Fine Arts Faculty Council, Studio Arts Department Council, Studio Arts Visiting Artist Committee, Studio Arts Curriculum Committee for Intermedia, and the CUPFA Professional Development Committee (prior to becoming VP Professional Development). In addition to overseeing the distribution of Professional Development and Small Claims funds, she promotes part-time faculty through such activities as MicroTalks, MicroLinks, CUPFA Special Project Awards and the creation of promotional videos. In addition, Oades has organized many cross-faculty events and workshops in Studio Arts and initiated a series of in-depth interviews with part-time faculty in <em>The Concordian</em>.</p><p>Oades looks forward to continuing to build an exciting environment that enhances part-time faculty\u2019s research and highlights our abilities both within and outside of the University.<br/>\nE:\xa0<a href="mailto:lorraine.oades@cupfa.org">lorraine.oades@cupfa.org</a><a href="mailto:locupfa@gmail.com" rel="nofollow noopener noreferrer" target="_blank"><br/>\n</a>T: 514-848-2424 ext. 3691</p><hr/><p><strong><strong><strong><strong><strong><img alt="" class="alignleft" height="114" src="http://cupfa.org//wp-content/uploads/2012/01/june_bio.jpg" title="June" width="114"/></strong></strong></strong>J<strong><strong>une Riley</strong><br/>\n</strong></strong></strong><em>Treasurer</em></p><p>I am June Riley, Treasurer of CUPFA since 2011.\xa0I have been part of the CUPFA Negotiations Team since 2014 helping to settle our 2012-2015 and 2015-2017 Collective Agreements.\xa0My duties as Treasurer include care and custody of Association funds and securities, including co-management of our strike fund \u2013 worth just over $2 million \u2013 along with our professional management team at Raymond James.
