Please leave a small blank space for the stamp on your poster.\xa0Posters may\xa0be brought to CSU Reception (SGW: H-711; LOY: SC-115) to be approved and stamped. Please note that posters without\xa0a stamp will be taken down. No more than 75 posters can be stamped for a single event (this number is reduced to 25 during CSU elections).</span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;">For more information, you are encouraged to review Concordia's <a href="http://www.concordia.ca/content/dam/common/docs/policies/official-policies/SG-1.pdf">"Policy on the Display of Posters"</a>.</span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;"><a id="newsletter" name="newsletter"><strong>\xa0</strong></a><strong>\xa0</strong></span><br/>\n\xa0</p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:18px;"><strong>CSU LIVE Newsletter</strong></span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;">Need help reaching out to the student body?\xa0The Concordia Student Union sends\xa0out the\xa0CSU LIVE newsletter\xa0weekly\xa0to every undergraduate\xa0at Concordia (that's over 35,000 people!). If you need help promoting an upcoming event, send all pertinent details\xa0(time, place, duration, etc.) along with a short description (2 sentences)\xa0of the event and a square\xa0image to illustrate it to our Communications Coordinator at <a href="mailto:communications@csu.qc.ca">communications@csu.qc.ca</a>\xa0and we will include it in our CSU LIVE newsletter. The submission\xa0deadline is every Wednesday and the newsletter is sent out by Sunday.\xa0</span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;">Take note that this works as a first come first served system &amp; that your post might not be included if it is sent too last minute or if the information sent is incomplete.\xa0</span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;">If you have questions about the LIVE Newsletter, email our Communications Coordinator at <a href="mailto:communications@csu.qc.ca">communications@csu.qc.ca</a>.</span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;"><a id="tabling" name="tabling"><strong>\xa0</strong></a></span><br/>\n\xa0</p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:18px;"><strong>Tabling</strong></span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;">The CSU oversees the use of the four tables located on the Hall building's Mezzanine. They can set you up with free web hosting using the platform of your choosing (Wordpress, Drupal, etc.).</span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;">Please note that all club websites must state that the club is \u201ca student group of the Concordia Student Union.\u201d</span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;"><a id="socialmedia" name="socialmedia"><strong>\xa0</strong></a></span><br/>\n\xa0</p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:18px;"><strong>Social Media</strong></span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;">Many clubs use social media to advertise for their events and to recruit members. We recommend that you consider making a Facebook page and a Twitter account\xa0for your club in order to ensure the greatest degree of communication and presence with your members.\xa0If your club has social media accounts, be sure to include them in your club description so they appear when students view your club\u2019s info.</span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;"><a id="mailinglist" name="mailinglist"><strong>\xa0</strong></a><strong>\xa0</strong></span><br/>\n\xa0</p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:18px;"><strong>Mailing List</strong></span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;">Clubs are advised to keep a mailing list of their members for easy communication and promotion of events. <a href="http://mailchimp.com">Mailchimp</a> is a free mailing list service that clubs can use.</span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;"><a id="club-mail-folder" name="club-mail-folder"></a></span><br/>\n\xa0</p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:18px;"><span style="line-height: 22.4px;"><b>Club Mail Folders</b></span></span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size: 14px; line-height: 22.4px;">The CSU provides spaces for each club and association to receive mail, faxes, and messages at either the SGW or Loyola campus. Your club's name needs to be marked clearly on the received fax, otherwise it will be considered junk and will be discarded.</span></p>\n\n<p class="rteindent1"><span style="font-size:16px;">Fax prices:</span><br/><span style="font-size:14px;">Received faxes: $0.10 per page<br/>\nLocal faxes: $0.25 per page<br/>\nCanada &amp; US: $0.50 per page<br/>\nInternational: $2.00 per page</span></p>\n\n<p class="rteindent1"><span style="font-size:14px;">Note: Clubs will need to pay for faxes upfront and\xa0in cash at reception.</span></p>\n\n<p><a id="comms-office" name="comms-office"></a><br/>\n\xa0</p>\n\n<p><strong><span style="font-size:18px;">The Comms Office</span></strong></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;">The Clubs Communications office is located in H-725, adjacent to the CSU office. The budget given to your club at the start of the year is put into this internal account, and you will also deposit any revenue you generate from events, projects, and sponsorships into this account. Unused budget funds do not carry over to the next academic year, but other deposited funds generated from external sponsors and events\xa0will remain.</span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;"><a id="requesting-budget" name="requesting-budget"></a></span><br/>\n\xa0</p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:18px;"><strong>Requesting a Budget</strong></span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;">Every year, clubs are required to submit a budget proposal so the Clubs Committee can\xa0issue club budgets for the year. Clubs must use the <a href="https://csu.qc.ca/content/download/clubs/club-budget-proposal-template">budget proposal template</a>\xa0provided by the CSU, as budget proposals\xa0other formats will not be accepted. Clubs will be notified of their allocated budget at the yearly Club Orientation, an event held in late September or early October that provides training to club execs on how to run a club. Completed budget proposals must be uploaded at the bottom of the club\u2019s registration page on the CSU website before the first day of classes of the Fall semester. Clubs that submit their budget proposals after this deadline may receive a reduced amount and may have to wait longer to receive their budget.</span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;"><a id="using-club-funds" name="using-club-funds"></a></span><br/>\n\xa0</p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:18px;"><strong>Using Club Funds</strong></span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;">The main way to use the money in your club account is to pay for an expense out-of-pocket and then get reimbursed by submitting a requisition form. To be reimbursed for out-of-pocket expenses, you should fill out a <a href="https://csu.qc.ca/download/requisitions/clubs-requisition-form">Clubs Requisition Form</a>\xa0on a computer, print it and have it signed by your club\u2019s two signing officers, attach the original receipts for all purchases (we will not accept debit or credit receipts)* and attach printed bank statements for all purchases made with debit or credit (you may blank out any personal or other information from these statements), and submit it to the CSU\u2019s Front Desk. You can also indicate on the form whether you would like to pick up the completed cheque at the CSU office or if you would like it mailed to you. After submitting a requisition form, you can expect a delay of approximately 2-3 weeks for it to be processed.</span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;">If a given expense is too large to pay out of pocket, you may have the organization or venue send an invoice directly to the CSU.\xa0For invoices, you should first come speak to the Internal Affairs Coordinator to ensure that you have enough money in your club budget, and that it is alright for us to accept the invoice. Once you obtain approval, the business or venue that you are dealing with can send an invoice for the expense directly to the CSU as long as it is clearly indicated that the expense is for your club.</span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;">*If you have misplaced the original receipts, you may ask to use\xa0printed debit or credit bank statements to verify\xa0the expenses. From their website:</span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;">"CCSL makes funds available to support projects that enhance or contribute to the quality of student life at Concordia. Events, projects, or initiatives that enhance student life are ones that seek to positively raise awareness or engage the student body in a social, political, educational, or cultural context. The fund is designed to support innovative and unique activities rather than ongoing expenses of student groups and initiatives."</span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;">Guidelines, application forms, and information about deadlines for the CCSL SPF can be found <a href="https://www.concordia.ca/students/beyond-the-classroom/ccsl/special-project-fund.html">here</a>. Filled out application forms should be dropped off at the Dean of Students Office (H-637 at SGW, or AD-121 at Loyola).</span></p>\n\n<p><br/><span style="font-size:16px;"><i>External Sponsors</i></span><br/><span style="font-size:14px;">If you are hosting an event around the University, there are always stores, hotels, cafes, and restaurants that are looking for ways to advertise to students. Offering them a sponsorship package and free advertisement during your event will encourage these companies to give you money and invest in your club. Please talk to the Internal Affairs Coordinator if you need any help making a sponsorship package.</span></p>\n\n<p><span style="font-size:14px;">If you receive a sponsorship, have the sponsor make the cheque out to CSU with the name of your club in the\xa0<em>Memo</em>\xa0section, then schedule a meeting with the Internal Affairs Coordinator to deposit the cheque in your club's internal account.
